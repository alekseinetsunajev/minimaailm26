Estonian fire and rescue — 1:43

Historical brigade trucks in 1:43 scale · Vintage enamel layout

Eesti | English | Language

History

Estonian fire and rescue services developed through municipal brigades, Soviet-era standardisation, and today’s national structures. Many vehicles on the road decades ago were imports or licence builds adapted to local climate, road width, and station practice.

This collection in 1:43 is a personal study of that lineage: how liveries, equipment, and body shapes changed while the same mission—protecting people and property—stayed constant.

Masterpiece

The centrepiece of the workshop is a single build chosen for ambition: the most complex conversion, the finest paint, or the rarest reference. It sets the bar for every other model on the shelf.

Here you would show the hero truck in full: why it matters historically, what broke or worked during construction, and which archives or photos proved the details.
